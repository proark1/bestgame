# Hive Wars — World & Story

A lore bible for the backyard. Every codex blurb, card power line, and
faction name currently in the game is kept coherent here. Anyone adding
a new unit, building, or quest should crack this open first — the
cosmology is tight, and the art pipeline reads best when the writer and
the painter share a map.

## The World — One Backyard

Everything in Hive Wars happens in a single overgrown garden: loose
stones, roots, leaf litter, a cracked terracotta pot, a dripping hose.
From a human window it's a patch of neglect. From the ants' angle it's
a continent.

Two layers cut through the world:

- **Surface** — sun, dew, birdsong, the long threat of the gardener's
  boot. Where walls are built and turrets bloom.
- **Underground** — humid, glowing with fungus, always warm. Where
  larvae hatch, the throne keeps its heart, and tunnels braid the map.

The **Queen Chamber** is the only structure that bridges both — a
surface turret wired to an underground throne. Every colony has one.
Lose it, and the story ends there.

## The Long Cold

Three winters ago a frost came down through the garden and did not lift
for a full season. Collectors froze on their stems. Tunnel junctions
collapsed under the weight of a thaw that never came. The old single
kingdom — one queen for every creature in the yard, a peace of sorts —
broke with the ice.

Smaller colonies emerged, each with a new queen, each claiming that
their line was the true continuation of the old throne. What had been
ritual became politics. What had been family became rivals. The
**Hive Wars** are the long bloom of that fracture.

## The Four Factions

The ancient alliance survived as four factions. They share a pheromone
alphabet (inherited from the pre-Cold colony) and a grudge, but each
lives and fights very differently.

### Ants — the Hosts

The player. A single queen. A dual-layer base. Buildings, income, walls,
the works. Every **Ant** in the codex is either part of the colony
proper — worker, soldier, digger, forager — or a defensive structure
grown from colony materials (leaf, pebble, spore, honey-dew).

The ants are the only faction that fields buildings. Everyone else is a
mercenary.

**Named ants:**

- **Worker Ant** — the backbone. Not a soldier by training. Runs through
  flame if the pheromone trail says so.
- **Soldier Ant** — heavy-jawed, bred for the breach. Takes a turret
  hit so three workers can reach the vault.
- **Dirt Digger** — a worker who got lost one winter and came back
  wearing the tunnels like armor. Ignores surface walls.
- **Forager** — reads a garden better than a map. Goes for dew, leaf,
  anything sweet. Trophies come second.

### Bees — the Wasp Court

The **wasp court** is a foreign power. Their hive sits two fence panels
over, across the neighbor's gutter. They sell air support in exchange
for sugar the ants can barely spare. No one trusts a wasp. Everyone
wants one on their side.

**Hired by the ants:**

- **Wasp** — flying sniper. Glass cannon. Cuts a path over a defensive
  maze for an infantry line behind her.
- **Honey Tank** — three drones fused into one honey-soaked husk.
  Absorbs fire. If a honey tank reaches the vault, the raid is over.

### Beetles — the Stone Orchards

The **stone orchards** lie on the far side of the flagstone path. Where
the ants farm dew, the beetles farm ore. They arrived at the gates
after the Cold with carapaces they'd sharpened on pebbles for
generations and a proposition: hire us, or fight us. The ants hired
them. Most of them, anyway — a **rival beetle colony** still holds the
eastern tunnels and is the primary opponent in the bot raid.

**Hired by the ants:**

- **Shield Beetle** — forged carapace. Walks into the line of fire so
  the colony behind can work. Reduces turret damage around it.
- **Bomb Beetle** — explosive sacs on her back. Knows she only walks
  one trail. Grandma said aim for the biggest wall and don't flinch.
- **Roller** — tucks into a sphere and picks up speed on downhill
  trails. The longer the path, the harder she hits.

### Spiders — the Silk Court

The **spiders** do not speak the pheromone alphabet. They signal with
silk. Eight-legged, eight-eyed, and unfailingly polite at the
negotiating table, though no one is sure whether the politeness is
ritual or threat. They're the most expensive mercenaries in the yard.
They're also the best.

**Hired by the ants:**

- **Jumper** — crosses leaf walls like the walls are suggestions. Still
  hates pebble bunkers.
- **Web Setter** — lays silk as she walks. Anything in the thread
  slows to a crawl — useful when you want mushroom turret salvos to
  hit your own heavies by mistake.
- **Ambusher** — arrives in silence, leaves in silence. The queen
  never sees her come.

## The Pheromone Trail

The in-game "draw a path" gesture is the player speaking in pheromone.
Lore-wise, a raid is a **scripted song**: the player's queen composes a
sequence of trails for mercenary squads to follow, each trail carrying
a unit type, a priority, a destination. The mercenaries follow the
scent because they are paid to, not because they were born to. Spiders,
who don't read pheromone natively, follow their handler's silk bead
laid along the same line.

This is why your ants can deploy beetles, bees, and spiders from the
same deck: every unit on the field is reading (or approximating) an
**ant-made trail**. If the queen chamber falls, the trails go silent,
the mercenaries walk home, and the raid ends.

## The Buildings

The ant colony's buildings are grown, not built. Each one is a
biological machine with a particular role:

- **Queen Chamber** — the only two-layer structure. Surface turret
  rooted into an underground throne room. Tier gates every other
  building.
- **Dew Collector** — morning-dew petals piped down to the vault.
  Sugar income.
- **Larva Nursery** — warm, humid, softly glowing. New workers by the
  hundred. Underground only.
- **Sugar Vault** — storage cap. The deeper the vault, the thicker the
  armor.
- **Mushroom Turret** — a bioengineered puff-cap that spits spore darts
  at anything on a pheromone trail. The alchemist worker who bred it is
  remembered in every colony's poem.
- **Leaf Wall** — tightly folded chlorophyll armor. Cheap. Meant to
  funnel, not stop.
- **Pebble Bunker** — hauled from the creek bed by generations of
  workers. Built to outlast the colony that built it.
- **Tunnel Junction** — where surface meets underground. Workers pass
  freely in peacetime; in a raid, diggers use it to flank upward.
- **Dungeon Trap** — a sprung plate over a dart-vine shaft. Every
  raider thinks they know the layout. Every raider is wrong once.

## The Comic — "Three Springs Ago"

A short, six-panel sketch of the event that kicked off the current war.
Art can be generated from these beats in the same menuUi admin pipeline
as the card frame; they're sized 1:1 to a card portrait cell.

1. **The Last Warm Morning.** The old queen on the surface, eating
   dew. Her daughters at her feet. Sunlight on the terracotta pot.
2. **The Cold.** A single line of frost creeping across the garden.
   No living figures — just white.
3. **The Throne Splits.** Inside the underground chamber, a hairline
   crack runs the length of the throne. Two daughters staring at each
   other across it.
4. **The Wasp at the Gate.** A wasp hovering at the chamber mouth,
   one leg held out, holding a contract. The queen's guards on either
   side, jaws tense, unmoving.
5. **The Pact.** An ant queen and a beetle chieftain pressing seal-
   rings together over a leaf, silhouetted against a torch. A spider
   in the corner of the frame, watching, silent.
6. **The First Raid.** An opposing hive in the distance. The player's
   queen standing at her chamber mouth, pheromone whorls rising from
   her antennae, the rival base visible through the leaf-fringe.

## Writing style guide

When you add a new codex entry:

- **Role** — three to five words, present tense, verb-forward. ("Burst
  assassin", "Ranged defense", "Cheap blocker".)
- **Story** — 2–3 sentences. Anchor the unit to the world (a place,
  a season, a relationship). Don't explain stats. Let voice carry the
  faction.
- **Power** — 1–2 sentences. State the mechanical edge and one usage
  hint. Never contradict the live sim stats — this is the player's
  in-fiction reference, not marketing copy.
- Avoid naming the *player*. The codex is in-universe; it's written by
  the colony's scribe, not the game designer.

## Game-design review (as of main @ ce0fd79)

Taking stock of whether the systems currently wired in *make sense* and
*feel fair*, reading from `shared/src/sim/stats.ts`, `RaidScene.ts`'s
hardcoded bot, and the codex powers.

### What works

- **Dual-layer base** is the single strongest idea: it gives tunnel
  junctions, dirt diggers, and the queen chamber real mechanical
  meaning that the lore also leans on.
- **Deterministic sim + seed 0xc0ffee** means every raid is
  reproducible. Replays and raid-submit round-tripping benefit.
- **Power-line taxonomy** — role / story / power — reads cleanly on
  a card and gives the writer a 3-slot budget per unit. Easy to keep
  consistent.
- **Mercenary framing** justifies the mixed unit deck (ants + beetles +
  bees + spiders) without a Frankenstein roster. Cosmetically and
  narratively clean.

### Balance observations

Pulled from `UNIT_STATS`, DPS = `attackDamage × 30 / cooldownTicks`:

| Unit | HP | DPS | Range | Notes |
|------|----|-----|-------|-------|
| WorkerAnt | 20 | 3.3 | 0.6 | intended swarm; fine |
| SoldierAnt | 40 | 7.5 | 0.8 | solid anchor; fine |
| DirtDigger | 30 | 4.0 | 0.5 | utility (dig); fine |
| Forager | 15 | 3.0 | 0.5 | glass; fine if loot multiplier real |
| Wasp | 25 | 5.0 | **2.5** | flying + range 2.5 is the map; may be over-tuned vs turrets |
| HoneyTank | 80 | 6.7 | 0.8 | tank; fine |
| ShieldBeetle | 120 | 4.0 | 0.6 | tank; aura (per codex) not yet visible in stats.ts |
| BombBeetle | 30 | — | 0.4 | one-shot 25 dmg; fine |
| Roller | 50 | 6.7 | 0.7 | needs long trail; fine |
| **Jumper** | 35 | **9.5** | 0.6 | highest sustained DPS **plus** wall-hop. Likely dominant. |
| WebSetter | 25 | 0.75 | 2.0 | pure utility; fine |
| **Ambusher** | 30 | 12.0 | 0.7 | highest DPS **plus** first-strike-triple per codex. Explosive vs queen. |

Two outliers flagged:

1. **Jumper** is currently the highest-DPS ant-recruitable unit **and**
   it ignores leaf walls. The codex tries to balance that with "medium
   HP, medium damage — value is positioning" but the numbers don't back
   it up. Options: drop base DMG to 5, *or* lower HP to 25 so
   turret focus fire punishes the skip.
2. **Ambusher** with a triple-damage first strike is effectively 36 HP
   on target on first hit, which one-shots most turrets and half-kills
   the queen chamber off a single deploy. Pair the triple clause with
   a cooldown (e.g. only once per raid) or scope it to *non-queen*
   targets.

Neither bonus is implemented in `stats.ts` yet — both exist only in the
codex blurb. Until they are, the written power is ahead of the sim.
Flag for the next combat-systems pass.

### Fairness / "makes sense"

- **Defender has no raid-time agency** (Clash-style); that's the
  designed loop. Fine.
- **Unit deployment appears free** (no per-deploy cost in
  `RaidScene.DECK`). Count limits bound the swarm but nothing
  punishes throwing everyone immediately. Consider adding a pheromone
  budget (regenerates over time) if matches start feeling like
  instant all-ins.
- **Bot base `faction: 'Beetles'` uses an all-Ants building roster**
  in `RaidScene.ts`. Cosmetically odd — either give the bot a true
  beetle kit (visually differentiated buildings), or set
  `faction: 'Ants'` to reflect what's on the board. The lore framing
  above ("rival beetle colony") argues for the former eventually.
- **Loot vs. HP match-up** — a SugarVault drops 500 sugar on destroy
  vs. its 350 HP. A Forager can take that with a small escort. Feels
  right for a 90-second raid.

### Suggested follow-ups

1. Implement ShieldBeetle's 60% aura in `combat.ts`.
2. Implement Ambusher first-strike-triple and Jumper wall-hop.
3. Decide whether those two need stat cuts once the bonuses land.
4. Reconcile `RaidScene.BOT_BASE.faction` with its building set.
5. Consider a pheromone budget / deploy cost.

None of these block today's playability — the raid is winnable and the
codex reads cleanly — but they're the cracks to patch before combat
depth can grow further.
