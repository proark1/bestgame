Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Hive Wars is a browser game. We collect the minimum data needed to run the game and keep your progress safe. This page explains what we store, why, and how to remove it.

What we store

  • A device identifier. Generated locally in your browser the first time you play. It's a random string; it's not linked to your name or email.
  • Your game state. Colony layout, units, resources, raid history, trophies. Attached to your device identifier.
  • Optional account info. If you create an account to save progress across devices, we store the display name and a password hash. We never ask for your real name or address.
  • Server logs. Anonymized IP + request path, retained up to 14 days for abuse protection and debugging.

What we don't do

  • We don't sell your data.
  • We don't use third-party advertising trackers.
  • We don't read your contacts, photos, or microphone.

Cookies + local storage

The game uses localStorage to remember your settings (sound on/off, text size, color-blind palette) and your session token. It does not use tracking cookies. Clearing your site data will reset those.

Analytics

We use a lightweight product-analytics tool to understand which features players use (session start, raid completion, retention). Events are sent without your name or email — only the device identifier and the event name. You can disable analytics in the in-game Settings menu.

Deleting your data

Email us at privacy@hivewars.example with the device identifier shown in Settings → About, and we'll delete everything we hold against it within 30 days.
